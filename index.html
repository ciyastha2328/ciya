MyBook Reviews

Here are reviews of some of the book that i have read this year.

The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini is a gripping novel about friendship, betrayal, and redemption. Set in Afghanistan, it follows Amir and Hassan, two boys from different social classes, whose bond is tested by Amir's desire for his father's approval and a subsequent act of betrayal. As Afghanistan undergoes political upheaval, Amir's journey toward adulthood becomes a quest for redemption. Hosseini's evocative writing and rich historical context make this a deeply moving and thought-provoking read..

The Kite Runner

The Palace of Illusions by Chitra Banerjee Divakaruni is a captivating retelling of the Mahabharata from Draupadi's perspective. This feminist take offers a fresh view of the epic, portraying Draupadi as a strong, complex woman navigating a world of magic, power, and societal constraints.The lyrical prose and rich narrative make it a compelling and thought-provoking read.

The Palace of Illusions

Before the Coffee Gets Cold by Toshikazu Kawaguchi is a charming and thought-provoking novel set in a small Tokyo caf√© where patrons can travel back in time. The story revolves around four customers who revisit pivotal moments in their lives, each bound by the rule that they must return before their coffee gets cold. Kawaguchi's gentle storytelling and focus on themes of love, regret, and second chances create a heartwarming and reflective read.
